AWARE

Adaptive Well-Being & Reflection Engine for Healthcare Workers

What is AWARE?

AWARE is an Intelligent User Interface (IUI) designed to help healthcare workers pause and reflect during demanding workdays. It offers short, adaptive check-ins grounded in well-being science using concepts from the Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI) and the Professional Fulfillment Index (PFI).

AWARE helps users notice emotional patterns, breathe, and ground themselves without judgment.

The Story of AWARE

Imagine a nurse moving quickly between tasks, patient care, and documentation. They have little time to reflect on how they feel. AWARE is designed for short, restorative moments where users can check in with themselves.

1Starting the Day
A gentle login welcomes the user.

2Warm Welcome
Science-backed messages and calming imagery help center the user.

3Personalization
The “Know Yourself” micro-assessment refines coping-style modeling.

4Daily Check-In
Users share stress levels and choose their reflection depth.

5Adaptive Support
AWARE adapts tone and guidance based on the user model and relational memory.

6Mixed-Initiative Dialogue
AWARE offers deeper prompts while letting the user stay in control.

7Pattern Insights
Users can view stress trends and coping signals.

8Closing the Moment
AWARE ends each check-in with supportive gratitude.

Core Principles Behind AWARE

  • User Modeling: Infers coping style and tone preference.
  • Relational Memory: References past meaningful entries.
  • Adaptivity: Tone, content, and depth adjust dynamically.
  • Mixed Initiative: Provides support while allowing user control.
  • Scientific Foundation: Grounded in validated MBI and PFI concepts.

Scientific Foundations

  • Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI)
    Maslach, C., Jackson, S. E., & Leiter, M. P. (1996). Maslach Burnout Inventory Manual.
    Official reference: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/job.4030020205
  • Professional Fulfillment Index (PFI)
    Trockel, M. T., et al. (2018). A brief instrument to assess burnout and fulfillment.
    Official reference: Professional Fulfillment Index Self Assessment Tool
